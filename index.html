3. Ethical Dilemma

Scenario: Your organisation develops an AI-driven digital marketing system that collects user data, analyses behaviour, and delivers targeted advertising. During testing, you discover that the system lacks clear user consent, collects excessive personal data, and may influence users in non-transparent ways. Despite these risks, management insists on releasing the system to maximise profit.
Privacy

The system collects and processes personal data without clear and informed user consent, increasing the risk of misuse.

Security

Weak safeguards may expose sensitive user data to breaches, unauthorised access, or cyberattacks.

Manipulation

AI-driven targeting may influence user decisions unfairly, especially for vulnerable individuals.

Trust

Deploying such a system can damage public trust in both the organisation and digital technologies.

Critical evaluation: This dilemma highlights a conflict between short-term business profit and ethical responsibility. Although releasing the system may benefit the company financially at first, ignoring ethical concerns may create wider harm for users and weaken trust over time.

Stakeholder impact: Different stakeholders are affected in different ways. Users may experience privacy violations, manipulation, and loss of control over personal data. Companies may face legal penalties, public criticism, and financial loss. Professionals may damage their integrity if they knowingly support harmful systems.
Cause and effect: Privacy invasion can lead to loss of trust, loss of trust can lead to reputational damage, and reputational damage can lead to financial and legal consequences for the organisation.

6. Code of Ethics

  • Avoid harm
  • Respect privacy
  • Be honest and transparent
  • Consider societal impact
Follow ethics before profit.
ACM Code idea: Professionals should avoid harm, protect privacy, and consider how their work affects people and society. According to ACM (2018), computing professionals must prioritise the public good, which directly applies to this situation. However, newer issues like AI bias and automated decision-making show why ethical guidelines must continue to evolve.

10. Personal Solutions & Scenario Outcomes

This section presents three possible actions and the outcome of each decision.

Solution 1: Launch the system with no changes
  • Follow the manager’s instructions
  • Release the AI system immediately
  • Do not raise further objections
Step 1: Approve the unsafe system.
Step 2: Allow the company to launch quickly.
Step 3: Deal with problems only after users are affected.

Outcome: Short-term profit may increase, but users face privacy violations, loss of trust, and possible harm. Legal and ethical consequences may follow.

Solution 2: Report the issue and suggest fixes
  • Inform management of the risks
  • Recommend clearer user consent
  • Reduce invasive data collection
  • Strengthen security and fairness checks
Step 1: Present the ethical and technical risks.
Step 2: Suggest concrete improvements.
Step 3: Support a safer redesign before release.

Outcome: Release may be delayed, but the system becomes safer and more transparent, improving trust and reducing long-term risks.

Solution 3: Escalate and refuse unsafe deployment
  • Raise the issue to senior leadership or ethics officers
  • Refuse to approve the unsafe system
  • Protect users despite pressure
Step 1: Document all risks formally.
Step 2: Escalate beyond direct management.
Step 3: Block deployment until ethical standards are met.

Outcome: Although this may create short-term conflict in the workplace, it best protects users, upholds professional ethics, and prevents major long-term harm.

Best choice: Solution 3 prioritises ethics, safety, and long-term trust over short-term gain. Although it may be difficult in the short term, it is the most responsible and professionally justified response.

11. Conclusion

Data science and AI can help the world, but only when professionals use them ethically, protect privacy, and make responsible decisions. This topic shows that technical skill alone is not enough; ethical judgment is equally important in the modern digital world.
